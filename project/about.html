Pokémon Go

Niantic, Inc. was the initial developer of Pokémon GO and the original owner of this game, which is based in San Francisco, and is considered to be one of the first mobile games based on augmented reality (AR). In 2016, Niantic produced in collaboration with The Pokémon Company and Nintendo their Pokémon GO, which instantaneously became a worldly hit. Nevertheless, in 2025 Niantic sold its games unit such as Pokémon GO to Scopely, a significant mobile game publisher and sponsored by the Saudi Arabia Public Investment Fund, which was sold off at $3.5 billion. Under this transformation, Scopely is the new owner and developer of Pokémon GO, and Niantic has been busy with its geospatial and augmented reality (AR) platform, which now operates under a new name Niantic Spatial.


Our Group

We are The Ascended. In this course, Web Design with Client-Side Scripting, we are composed of three students and planned to complete our final project, which is the redesign of Pokémon GO Web Store. We will make the process of shopping easier and more visual and quick to respond by the user among the Pokémon GO consumers. As would-be web developers and designers, we are pooling our HTML, CSS, UI/UX, and creativity together to give a new functioning face to one of the most well-liked mobile gaming fronts of the world. In collaboration, we are striving to take it a step further and basically rise the bar of what a student project can be.
